Why a Sensory Environment Helps

De-stress

Engaging sensory processes can help calm an overstimulated mind by providing a moment of comfort and calm where the mind is absorbing and refocusing attention. Additionally, a sensory environment can help those withdrawn feel more comfortable in their surroundings allowing for positive interaction.

Sensory stimulation

Encouraging exploration of their environment can help individuals have a better understanding of the world around them and their ability to interact and react within it.

Enhance learning through play

Using a Montessori approach allows for self-directed activity, hands-on learning, and collaborative play. Sensory play helps children develop their senses, encourages problem-solving, and helps build nerve connections within the brain. Engaging different areas of the brain helps children absorb and retain more information.

Improve balance, movement and spatial orientation

Use of sensory rooms helps develop visual processing, fine and gross motor skills, and facilitates day-to-day living. It is also highly beneficial to individuals with physical difficulties.

Develop communication skills

For individuals with autism, verbal and non-verbal communication can be a challenge. Interactive sensory areas can help withdrawn individuals to engage. Focusing on the use of sensory toys with sound can also help promote vocalisation.

Improve focus

When individuals are over-stimulated (common in ADHD), sensory equipment helps draw attention back to the task at hand. This can help people cope in environments where calm and concentration are key.

Promote socialisation

Using a sensory room with others in a calm and stress-free environment helps develop social skills among peers and builds understanding of how actions affect others.
